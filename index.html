Toyota Innova Crysta facelift launched at Rs 16.26 lakh

The made-for-India updated Toyota Innova Crysta gets almost the same set of styling changes that were seen on the Indonesian-spec model revealed last month. The new Innova Crysta gets a slightly lager grille with a thick chrome surround and more slats than before. While the headlamps are exactly the same as the outgoing model, they now get chrome extensions on the grille. The front bumper gets a new design with larger turn indicator housings and round fog lamps that will be LED units on higher variants. Additionally, the front bumper has a sharper look, and comes with a blacked-out chin that has subtle chrome bits. A faux skid plate that was seen on the Indonesian-spec car does not seem to have made it to the Indian version. Additionally, the updated Innova Crysta gets new-look 16-inch diamond-cut alloy wheels that seem to be unique for the Indian model.

2020 Mercedes-AMG GLC 43 Coupe review

In 2017, Mercedes-AMG introduced the GLC 43 coupe, and being an AMG, it packed strong performance into an SUV-coupé body style. After giving the GLC SUV a mid-life facelift in 2019, and introducing a ‘tamer’ versions of the coupe-SUV in 2020, the sportier AMG version gets a mid-life update. But the bigger talking point here is that this is the first AMG to be locally assembled at Mercedes’ Chakan plant in Maharashtra. So, unlike the previous car, which was a full import and attracted high taxes, this one will receive some tax sops, and is likely to be more affordable than before. Priced at Rs 76.70 lakh (ex-showroom), this AMG costs just a bit more in 2020, as it did in 2017 (Rs 74.80 lakh).
